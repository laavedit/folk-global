Our Mission

What Drives Us

We uphold the values of indigenous practices, local knowledge, and cultural heritage as foundations for building resilient societies.

🏛️

Heritage Conservation

Protecting and restoring traditional architectural heritage, including Kathkuni temples and wooden structures of the Himalayas, using indigenous construction techniques and GIS documentation.

📚

Indigenous Knowledge Systems

Documenting and preserving intangible cultural heritage — from bamboo weaving and wool crafts to traditional water mills (Gharat) and oral traditions of mountain communities.

🌍

Climate & Disaster Resilience

Integrating traditional ecological knowledge with modern science to strengthen community resilience against floods, earthquakes, and climate-related disasters across South Asia.

Our Work

Projects & Initiatives

From temple restoration to disaster response, FOLK works where indigenous knowledge and modern resilience meet.

🕌
Heritage

Kathkuni Temple Restoration — Chehni Village

Restoring traditional wooden architecture in Tirthan Valley using indigenous Kathkuni techniques, with GIS mapping of 300+ heritage temples in Kullu district.

🚨
Disaster Response

Himachal Pradesh & Kerala Disaster Response

Providing rescue, relief, and situation mapping during the Kerala 2018 floods and Himachal Pradesh 2023 disaster, working alongside district administration.

📋
Documentation

Cultural Impact Assessment

Documenting damage to tangible and intangible cultural heritage following the 2023 disaster — religious practices, rituals, and traditional livelihoods in Kullu-Mandi.

🏫
Safety

School Safety Programs — Kullu

Structural assessments, hazard identification, disaster management plans, and awareness programs for students and staff in Kullu district schools.

💧
Environment

Natural Water Sources Mapping

Mapping indigenous water sources across Mandi and Manali regions to preserve traditional water management knowledge and support conservation.

🧶
Livelihoods

Traditional Craft & Knowledge Documentation

Preserving bamboo weaving, wool weaving, mirror making, and Gharat (water mill) practices as sustainable livelihoods and living cultural heritage.
