Our Work

Six active areas of work where indigenous knowledge meets modern disaster science to build resilient communities.

Project 01 · Heritage

Indigenous Heritage Protection — Kathkuni Temple Restoration

Location: Chehni Village, Tirthan Valley, Kullu, Himachal Pradesh

FOLK is restoring the Chehni Kothi Tower and surrounding Kathkuni-style temples using traditional wooden construction techniques passed down through generations. These ancient structures represent an irreplaceable record of Himalayan architectural heritage.

The project included GIS mapping of 300+ heritage temples across Kullu district, documenting not only the physical structures but also the intangible heritage — songs, rituals, and community practices — associated with each site.

Partner: Cultural Emergency Response (CER), Netherlands

Project 02 · Disaster Response

Disaster Response — Himachal Pradesh & Kerala

Kerala (2018) & Himachal Pradesh (2023)

FOLK deployed rapid response teams following the 2018 Kerala floods and the 2023 Himachal Pradesh multi-district disaster — one of the worst in the state's recent history.

Responsibilities included:

  • Situation reporting and needs assessment
  • Affected-area mapping and evacuation route planning
  • Safe zone identification and community coordination
  • Collaboration with district administration on relief logistics

Traditional ecological knowledge was integrated into route-finding and safe-zone assessment, demonstrating the practical value of indigenous knowledge in modern disaster response.

Project 03 · Documentation

Cultural Impact Assessment — Kullu-Mandi

Following the 2023 Himachal Pradesh disaster, FOLK conducted a detailed Cultural Impact Assessment across the Kullu-Mandi region — one of the first systematic assessments of disaster impact on intangible cultural heritage in the state.

The assessment documented the loss and damage to:

  • Religious practices, temple rituals, and festival traditions
  • Traditional livelihoods (farming, craft, pastoral activities)
  • Community memory and oral knowledge systems
  • Physical heritage structures linked to cultural practices

This work laid the groundwork for culturally sensitive disaster recovery planning in the region.

School Safety

School Safety Programs — Kullu District

Working with district administration to conduct structural hazard assessments of school buildings, develop school-level disaster management plans, and deliver safety awareness programs to students and staff. Builds a culture of preparedness from a young age.

💧
Environment

Natural Water Sources Mapping — Mandi & Manali

A community-led initiative to locate, document, and map traditional water sources (springs, streams, and channels) across Mandi and Manali. Preserves indigenous water management knowledge and supports sustainable resource planning in mountain communities.

🧶
Living Heritage

Traditional Craft & Knowledge Documentation

FOLK documents and promotes traditional Himalayan crafts — bamboo weaving, wool weaving, mirror making, and Gharat (water mill) practices — as both cultural heritage and sustainable livelihood sources. Connects artisans with markets and researchers.
