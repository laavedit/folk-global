Our Team

A diverse group of academics, practitioners, and community workers united by a commitment to living knowledge.

Leadership

Founders & Directors

MS
Co-Founder & Director

Madhusudan Singh

A climate change and disaster risk reduction professional with deep field experience across Nepal, Odisha, and Kerala. Madhusudan has worked on climate adaptation, emergency response, and community resilience at the intersection of policy and practice.

He participated in the Smithsonian Institution's Faculty and Curatorial Program (FAC) and has collaborated with national and international bodies including NDRF India, UNDP, and the European Commission.

M.Sc. Disaster Management — TISS UNDP India European Commission NDRF India Smithsonian Institution (FAC)
MM
Co-Founder & Executive Director

Mohini Mehta

A doctoral scholar in Sociology at Uppsala University, Mohini brings an interdisciplinary lens to FOLK's work, drawing on postcolonial feminism, identity politics, and food studies. Her MA degrees from Central European University (Hungary) and TISS (India) anchor her comparative South Asian perspective.

Beyond her academic work, Mohini is a spoken word poet and professional storyteller — skills she uses to communicate indigenous narratives to wider audiences.

PhD Sociology — Uppsala University MA — CEU Hungary MA — TISS India
AK
Director

Abhishek Kumar

A development professional specialising in ecosystem-based adaptation and climate resilience, Abhishek works at the frontline of Himalayan conservation. His expertise lies in integrating traditional ecological knowledge with modern scientific approaches to help communities adapt to environmental change.

He brings practical experience from across Himalayan communities and a rigorous academic grounding in disaster management from one of India's premier technical institutions.

M.Sc. Disaster Management — IIT Roorkee Ecosystem-based Adaptation Climate Resilience
